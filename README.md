# DeskBreak

**A free, lightweight Windows tray app that reminds you to stand up, stretch, and take care of yourself during long computer sessions.**

DeskBreak lives quietly in your system tray and sends gentle notifications so you don't lose track of time: stand up and stretch, rest your eyes, check your posture, or drink some water. No ads, no accounts, no data collection — just a simple nudge, right when you need it.

---

## Features

- 🏃 **Movement reminders** — stand up and stretch, with a specific exercise suggested each time (not just "get up")
- 👁️ **Eye rest reminders** — based on the well-known 20-20-20 rule, to reduce eye strain from long screen sessions
- 🪑 **Posture reminders** — quick checks for your back, monitor height, and leg position
- 💧 **Water reminders** — a simple nudge to stay hydrated during the day
- ⏱️ **Fully customizable intervals** — turn any reminder type on or off, and set how often it appears (in minutes)
- 📚 **Built-in exercise library** — short, concrete descriptions of stretches for the neck, shoulders, back, wrists, and legs
- 🚀 **Optional autostart** — launch automatically with Windows
- 🔒 **Free and private** — no ads, no telemetry, no paid tiers, no account required

## Installation

1. Download the latest installer: **`DeskBreak-Setup.exe`**
2. Run it and follow the on-screen steps (default options work fine for most people)
3. DeskBreak will start automatically after installation and appear in your system tray

> **Note about the Windows SmartScreen warning:** DeskBreak is an independent, free application without a paid code-signing certificate, so Windows may show a "Windows protected your PC" warning during installation. This is the standard behavior for any unsigned app — not a sign of a problem. Click **More info** → **Run anyway** to continue.

## System requirements

- Windows 10 or Windows 11 (64-bit)
- ~50 MB of free disk space
- No other dependencies — everything needed is bundled in the installer

## How to use

1. Look for the DeskBreak icon in your system tray (bottom-right corner of the taskbar; click the small arrow to show hidden icons if you don't see it)
2. Right-click the icon to open the menu:
   - **Settings** — enable/disable each reminder type and set its interval
   - **Exercise library** — browse all the exercises and tips DeskBreak can suggest
   - **Paused** — temporarily pause all reminders
   - **Exit** — close the app
3. That's it — keep working as usual, and DeskBreak will remind you at the right moments

## Uninstalling

Use **Settings → Apps → Installed apps** in Windows and remove DeskBreak like any other program, or run the uninstaller from the DeskBreak folder in your Start menu.

## Privacy

DeskBreak does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. All settings are stored locally on your own computer. There is no internet connection required for the app to function.
